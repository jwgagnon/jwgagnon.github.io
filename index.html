About

I am a masters student at McGill University working on queer theory and cyberculture in the English department. I completed my first class honours English Literature BA at McGill in 2020 under the supervision of Miranda Hickman.

This website features my ongoing academic and personal projects. My CV is available here.

Research areas: queer theory, phenomenology, narratology, media studies, DH, video games, media archaeology, internet studies, blockchain

News

  • February 2021: I am presenting my work "'A Chain of Transmission from the Past': Inheritance of HIV/AIDS in Multigenerational Historical Fiction " at the 2021 Post45 Graduate Symposium. This presentation was funded by a McGill GREAT Award.

  • June 2020: I was awarded the Allen A. Lang Essay Award for my essay "An Assault on Gay Memory”: Transmission of the AIDS Epidemic in Historical Fiction.

  • May 2020: I was selected as the recipient of the Shakespeare Gold Medal. The Medal was established in 1864 by citizens of Montreal to commemorate the three hundredth anniversary of the birth of Shakespeare.

  • May 2020: I have completed my honours thesis titled "Replacing the Political with the Personal": Myths of the Homophobic Homosexual. I was awarded the Algy Smillie Noad Memorial Prize for the best honours thesis in the deparment.

  • September 2019: I was chosen as the editor-in-chief for 2019-2020 of The Channel, McGill's Undergraduate Academic Journal.
